My Research Journey

My research journey as a graduate student in the lab of Prof. Ilja Siepmann at the University of Minnesota has been an exploration of the microscopic world of chemical systems. Guided by curiosity and a passion for understanding, I embarked on a journey that led me to unravel the secrets of molecular dynamics and phase behavior.

Exploring the Microscopic World

Journey into Supercritical Phase Equilibria

Aug. 2022-Present

My research took me into the supercritical realm of H2-H2O mixtures, where the boundaries between phases blur and molecules dance to their own tune. Through density functional theory (DFT)-based Monte Carlo simulations, I explored the phase behavior of these mixtures, unveiling the intricate dance of hydrogen and water molecules.

The Secrets of Electrolytes

Aug. 2021-Dec. 2022

My journey extended to the heart of Li-ion batteries, where electrolytes held the key to enhanced performance and safety. Collaborating with brilliant minds, I deciphered the structure and dynamics of LiTFSI electrolytes. This exploration revealed the fascinating world of solvation structures and the mechanics behind Li-ion transport.

Unlocking Zeolite Mysteries

Jan. 2022-Aug. 2022

The final chapter of my journey led me to zeolites and their potential for methane storage. With innovative techniques, I built a comprehensive database and ventured into the world of machine learning. Together, we uncovered the zeolites best suited for methane adsorption, opening new possibilities for energy storage.
